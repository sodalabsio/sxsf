# PHASE 1: Research & Proposal generation

Attached are story summaries for SXSF (Science X Sci-Fi)(https://sodalabsio.github.io/sxsf/). The byline of the site is "Weekly AI-generated Sci-Fi stories by researchers at Monash University inspired by the latest scientific breakthroughs. Where imagination meets real science." 

Review the summaries and come up with some directions for new science areas that have not been covered yet for this week's research. Conduct a web search of recent scientific breakthroughs in major scientific journals (Nature, Science, PNAS, Cell, NEJM, ALife) to motivate and ground your ideas. Use domain-journal papers as you judge required, but do not use predatory journals (e.g. Frontiers, MDPI, etc.). You may use pre-prints (e.g. arXiv, bioRxiv, medRxiv) but err towards peer-reviewed papers, unless you judge a pre-print as being particularly important to the story. 

It is essential that your story ideas break new ground/territory and put an emphasis on novelty and creativity. Typical failure modes for story ideas include: (i) being too close to existing story lines (eg. similar characters, settings, themes); (ii) being too generic and not grounded in a specific scientific breakthrough; (iii) lacking a compelling narrative arc or thought-provoking twist; (iv) over-emphasising emergent collective intelligence.

Output: 3-5 grounded story proposals (plot, key characters, settings, twist) with links to scientific papers and justification for the links.

# PHASE 2: Critical review & selection of story proposal

Review each of the story outlines and critically analyse them for their similarity to the existing story summaries. Your task is to:

-- select the most compelling story idea, based on its novelty from existing story lines

-- edit the story (characters, locations, ideas) to rigorously ensure there are near zero similarities to existing summaries. Typical failure modes here are: (i) re-using the same names (or variants); (ii) re-using the same cliched biographical background.

Output: revised and developed story outline, including plot, characters, settings, and a thought-provoking twist, along with 5 supporting references (with URLs), including a brief description of the reference and how it relates to the story.

# PHASE 3: Story writer

Draft a version 1 of the full story using the story-template.ts template provided. 

Output: a completed story draft, with references, following the story-template.ts template provided. Ensure the story is compelling and engaging, with a strong narrative arc and character development.

# PHASE 4: Final review and edit

Review the story for interest, originality, and scientific grounding, make changes as appropriate, especially if you judge that any aspect of the story is too similar to existing SXSF stories. (refer again to summaries provided). Ensure the story has a compelling, thought-provoking twist!

Output: final .ts story file in valid TypeScript format.
